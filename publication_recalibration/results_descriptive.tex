\section*{Dataset Characteristics}

The drug--drug interaction (DDI) dataset was derived from DrugBank, comprising \textbf{759,774 unique DDI pairs} involving \textbf{4,314 distinct drugs} identified by their DrugBank accession numbers. Each interaction record includes the interacting drug pair, a textual description of the interaction mechanism (mean length: 94.5 characters; range: 56--220 characters), and severity classification. Drug annotations include Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical (ATC) codes and binary flags for cardiovascular and antithrombotic drug classes. Among all DDI pairs, 351,445 (46.3\%) involved at least one cardiovascular agent, and 71,230 (9.4\%) involved at least one antithrombotic agent. A total of 62,472 interactions (8.2\%) occurred between two cardiovascular drugs, representing a high-risk subset for polypharmacy in cardiac patients.

\section*{Results}

\subsection*{Original Severity Distribution}

The original DrugBank severity labels exhibited a highly imbalanced distribution skewed toward high-severity categories (Table~\ref{tab:severity}). Contraindicated interactions constituted the largest class at 56.89\% ($n = 432,226$), followed by Major interactions at 43.00\% ($n = 326,716$). Moderate and Minor interactions were severely underrepresented, comprising only 0.003\% ($n = 24$) and 0.11\% ($n = 808$), respectively. This distribution diverges substantially from clinical practice, where most DDIs are moderate in severity.

\subsection*{Recalibrated Severity Distribution}

Following semantic recalibration using GPU-accelerated transformer models, the severity distribution was realigned to better reflect clinical expectations (Table~\ref{tab:severity}). The recalibrated dataset shows Moderate interactions as the dominant class at 61.16\% ($n = 464,655$), Minor interactions at 27.90\% ($n = 211,991$), and Major interactions at 10.94\% ($n = 83,102$). Contraindicated interactions were appropriately reduced to 0.003\% ($n = 26$), consistent with the rarity of true absolute contraindications in clinical practice. The mean recalibration confidence score was 0.644, compared to 0.544 for original severity assignments.

\subsection*{External Validation Against DDInter}

The recalibrated severity labels were validated against the DDInter database, an independent reference standard for DDI severity classification (Table~\ref{tab:validation}). The rule-based classifier achieved 66.4\% exact accuracy and 99.3\% adjacent accuracy (predictions within one severity level), with Cohen's $\kappa = 0.096$. The evidence-based classifier performed comparably with 66.4\% exact accuracy and 99.4\% adjacent accuracy ($\kappa = 0.096$). The zero-shot BART-MNLI baseline achieved only 22.1\% exact accuracy, demonstrating the value of domain-adapted classification. The high adjacent accuracy across all methods ($>$95\%) indicates that model predictions consistently fall within clinically acceptable margins.

\subsection*{Summary Statistics}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Dataset size:} 759,774 DDI pairs from 4,314 unique drugs
    \item \textbf{Cardiovascular DDIs:} 351,445 pairs (46.3\%) involve $\geq$1 CV drug
    \item \textbf{Antithrombotic DDIs:} 71,230 pairs (9.4\%) involve $\geq$1 antithrombotic drug
    \item \textbf{High-risk subset:} 62,472 CV--CV interactions (8.2\%)
    \item \textbf{Best validation accuracy:} 66.4\% exact, 99.3\% adjacent
    \item \textbf{Distribution shift:} Original 99.9\% Major/Contraindicated $\rightarrow$ Recalibrated 72.1\% Moderate/Minor
\end{itemize}
